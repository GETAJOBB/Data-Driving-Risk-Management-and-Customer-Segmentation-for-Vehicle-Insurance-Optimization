Data Driving Risk Management and Customer Segmentation for Vehicle Insurance Optimization¶

1. Background¶

The Automotive Insurance Analytics program is designed to optimize many operational areas of automotive insurance, including risk assessment and claims management, customer segmentation, education, and security initiatives. Auto insurance is an important part of personal financial planning because it protects you from financial losses caused by accidents, thefts, and other disasters. Insurers, on the other hand, must combine profitability with competitive premiums. To investigate this complex dynamic, the study employed a vast dataset that included precise information about insurance plans, customer demographics, vehicle characteristics, and claims history.

Targeting Young and Inexperienced Drivers: Since younger drivers, particularly those newly licensed, are more prone to accidents due to lack of experience, the company can develop safety education programs tailored to young drivers based on their age and driving experience. As a risk reduction strategy, higher premium rates can be applied to drivers in the early stages of their driving careers.

Incentives for Young Drivers: The company can offer incentives, such as premium discounts, to young drivers who complete safe driving courses. This can encourage better driving habits and reduce risk.

Retaining Experienced Drivers: To retain experienced drivers, who are generally more cautious on the road, the company could provide special bonuses or discounts. For example, loyalty rewards can be given to drivers with a claim-free record over a specified period.

3.1 Correlation of Risk Factor and Insurance Claim¶

In [145]:
risk_factors = ['speeding_violations', 'past_accidents', 'duis', 'outcome']

correlation_matrix = data[risk_factors].corr()

sns.heatmap(correlation_matrix, annot=True, cmap='coolwarm')
plt.title('Correlation between risk factors and insurance claims')
plt.xticks(rotation=45, ha='right')
plt.show()

All risk variables (speeding violations, past accidents, and drunk driving) show a negative correlation with the outcome, indicating that a higher number of violations (speeding, accidents, or drunk driving) is associated with worse insurance claim outcomes. This negative relationship suggests that individuals with a poor history in these areas are more likely to face difficulties in filing claims or receive lower compensation.

The correlation between past accidents and outcomes is -0.31, indicating a negative relationship between accident history and insurance claim results. This implies that the more accidents a person has had in the past, the worse their claim outcomes are likely to be. This negative correlation highlights a significant adverse connection between accident history and claim outcomes. Even with this negative relationship, the link between past accidents and claim results remains, suggesting that the causes of prior accidents may exacerbate the claim outcomes.

Similarly, drivers with fewer or no overtaking accidents are more likely to file successful claims, while those with multiple overtaking accidents are more likely to face claim rejections. The greater the number of violations, the lower the likelihood of claim approval.

Drivers with fewer or no DUIs are more likely to have their claims approved, but those with multiple DUIs are more likely to have their claims denied. As with other violations, the more frequent the infractions, the lower the chances of claim approval.

If traffic violations, past accidents, or DUIs are found to be closely associated with claim outcomes, companies can prioritize prevention by offering driver safety education programs. Additionally, they can develop special premium plans for consumers with a good driving record.
